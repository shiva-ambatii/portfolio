Patient Connect

Product Context

Carestack is a cloud based Practice Management Software for dental practices located in USA.

CareStack provides solutions to these practices for various needs such as scheduling, treatment planning, billing, insurance payment collection, patient engagement etc

Feature: Patient connect kiosk allows patients to self check-in and submit documents at the hospital

Kiosk is one of such offerings from CareStack that fall into the above mentioned Patient Engagement category. This solution enables the patients to complete the tasks such as check-in, documents signs etc. which otherwise have to be guided by the staff at practice. This reduces the workload on the staff and frees them up for more important tasks.

  • Self check-in for their appointments of the day.
  • Sign & submit the necessary documents for the treatments
  • To verify medical history details ( Allergies, medications, Surgeries etc)
  • Other tasks (editing personal information, adding a patient etc)

Since the patients use patient connect portal more often the same interface has been adapted to kiosk mode as well. So that the patients would find it easier to finish the required task.

Issues raised by the practices

Practices started complaining about the low task completion rate and overall low effectiveness of the kiosk. Several patients needed help from the staff to finish tasks on kiosk.

In order to understand the cause for these issues we went through different scenarios and generated some hypothesis some of which were later confirmed by practice staff during discussions.

Goal

To create a solution that effectively guides the patients to complete the tasks required by the practice i.e. sign the forms & check - in.

How?

1. Trimming the features

Most of the features in the patient connect are not essential for kiosk mode.

Removing / hiding the rarely used features. <10% used at kiosk

2. Contextual Information Architecture

To have better discoverability of the features the user is required to login after they select the task. This also allows patient to login with lesser data while checking in as the database for that days appointment is already filtered.

3. Creating an effective home screen with clear hierarchy of tasks to be completed

The approach was to provide a sort of to-do list for the patients when they walk into the practice and upon completion the patient would be logged out automatically unless the patient decides not to.

Home screen wireframes

Task completion flow

4. Making document signing easier

One of the problems identified with the current design is patients find it hard to sign on touch interface with a finger. To reduce this friction in the task completion, the idea is to capture the signature once per user per login and the subsequent documents can be signed with a single tap.

User feedback

The practices were happy with the overall approach and gave more inputs. A few more requirements have been identified during the interactions with users and different stakeholders at this point.

‚Üí Practices would like to also collect documents which are not associated with the appointment.

‚Üí Verify/ Edit personal and insurance details before check in.

Patients do not update their address, contact and other information promptly which would create problems for billing and collections after treatment is completed. Practices would like the information updated before treatments. This can be added as another task to the home screen.

‚Üí Ability to make few forms mandatory to check in

Since some practices did not want to mandate this. This is provided as a optional feature in their practice settings.

Modifications to the design

  • Adding basic information verification as task to the list.
  • Adding account details as settings.
